FAVORITE SHOWS

1. The Legend of Condor Hero (2001)
     It might seem like an odd choice considering the obvious low-budget production. But this hidden gem offers
a nicely paced story of two lovers, simply trying to get together. Each obstacle gets tougher, but the hero only
grows stronger from it. Somehow, depite the constant distance, they persevere their love even more.
Never got bored binging through all 75 episodes of LOCH. If you stick with it, the occassional, goofy animation
becomes funny and endearing.


2. The Walking Dead series
     One of the most famous TV shows, it shows the suspenseful journey of Rick Grimes, a former police officer
who would do anything for his family to live. The viewers would surely root for the protagonist and his company to
survive the odds. The first 6 seasons were the best in my opinion, but if you're an apocalyptic film fan like me,
you'd surely enjoy the entire series.


3. Breaking Bad series
    Another most acclaimed TV show, it tells the story of Walter White, an ingenious chemist who is clearly not
appreciated enough, break bad. Most of the time, it is sad and pitiful to see a person turn into a dredge of society,
but this process of change is one that would leave the viewer cheering for the protagonist to become the antagonist,
despite all the wrongdoing involved. It's about finding appreciation and satisfaction about oneself.


FAVORITE BOOKS

1. Man's Search For Meaning (1946)
     As an edgy teen, I was often nihilistic and pessimistic, trying to think about life's meaning. As most young people,
or most people in general, I was struggling with the question. This book inquires the same question, only this time it is
asked in one of the most horrific circumstances, in the concentration camp. The author, psychologist Viktor Frankl,
describes his experiences and his observations on how different people try to find meaning in such a situation. It is
one of the mysteries of life, and I highly recommend it if you are pondering about it.


2. No Longer Human (1973)
     A semi-autobiography about the immersive and depressing life of fictional character Oba Yozo. It boils down to the
inability to express oneself and how suffocating it can feel, leading to dark paths such as addiction for escapism.


3. Diary of a Wimpy Kid
     Not really my favorite anymore, but growing up, I used to be crazy about this book series. Whenever my family went to
the mall, the first thing that always came to my mind was go to National Bookstore and go hunting for the latest series
until I completed my collection. In less than a week, I would be done with one book and look forward to the next. In that time
when there were no other books of the series, I'd find other books as a substitute, but I enjoyed them as well nonetheless.


FAVORITE FOODS

1. Adobo
     The national dish of the Philippines. Everyone loves adobo. I grew up with two versions of it though: the traditional adobo &
adobo sa puti or white adobo. It's the Ilocano version. I prefer the white/vinegar version of it though most of the time because
it's the first dish my father has ever cooked me. Plus, the taste buds don't lie.


2. Pinakbet
     Another dish originating from the Ilocos region, Pinakbet to me is like an explosion of flavors in the mouth. The broth,
the texture of calabaza or winter squash, the unique cruchiness and consistency of okra or lady's fingers, the distinctive
taste of fermented fish or bagoong, and other vegetables, all harmonize with each other, accompanied with the fattiness
of small, pork bits.
